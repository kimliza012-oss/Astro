Mission

Advancing our understanding of cosmic structures through exploration and research.



SpaceX : Falcon Rescue Mission

  • Launch: March 14, 2025, at 23:03:48 UTC (7:03 PM EDT) atop a Falcon 9 from KSC LC -39A

  • Crew : Anne McClain (Commander, NASA), Nichole Ayers (Pilot, NASA), Takuya Onishi (JAXA) , Kirill Peskov (Roscosmos)

  • Purpose: Originally described as a “rescue,” it was a crew rotation. Two astronauts (Suni Williams & Butch Wilmore) were aboard ISS beyond planned duration due to Boeing Starliner issues; SpaceX brought relief crew to relieve them and return the extended crew aboard an operational Crew Dragon instead of Starliner.

  • Return : Stranded astronauts returned, splashdown March 18, 2025. Physical and psychological impacts of 9‑month stay were notable



SpaceX - Polaris Dawn

  • Launch (Expected): Late 2024 (Delayed from March 2023)

  • Mission Leader: Jared Isaacman (billionaire pilot, led Inspiration4)

  • Crew: Jared Isaacman (Commander) ,Scott Poteet (Pilot) ,Sarah Gillis (Mission Specialist) ,Anna Menon (Mission Specialist)

  • Spacecraft: Crew Dragon, launched via Falcon 9



ISRO - Chandrayaan - 3

  • Launch Vehicle: LVM3 (GSLV Mk III) on July 14, 2023, from Sriharikota

  • Components :

    • Propulsion Module (PM): Carries lander/rover into lunar orbit. After release, reentered high-Earth orbit to conduct Earth-observation payload “SHAPE”


    Vikram Lander: Improved with stronger landing legs, throttleable engines, hazard avoidance sensors, and redundancy for reliability


    Pragyan Rover: Six-wheeled, 26 kg. Delivered in-situ soil composition via APXS and LIBS sensors; range ~500 m


  • Landing: Precision soft landing near ~69.37°S, 32.35°E (“Statio Shiv Shakti”) on August 23, 2023



ISRO - INSAT - 3DS

  • Launch: February 17, 2024 at ~17:35 IST via GSLV-F14 (first flight of extended cryogenic stage CUS-15 with eco -fairing) from SDSC - SHAR

  • Satellite specs: Built on I-2K bus; mass ~2,275 kg. Expected lifespan ~10 years. Power ~1,164 W

  • Payloads:

    • Imager: 6 spectral channels (visible, SWIR, thermal, water vapor)


    Sounder: 19 vertical atmospheric profiles (temp/humidity/ozone)


    DRT: Data relay from ground stations


    SAS&R: Search and Rescue transponder for distress signals




NASA — JWST Operations

  • Launch: December 2021; full scientific operations began mid-2022

  • Major Discoveries (2023–2025):

    • Detected organic molecules in a galaxy nearly 12 billion light-years away (June 2023)


    Confirmed the most distant black hole merger in May 2024 (~740 million years after the Big Bang)


    Discovered a grand-design spiral galaxy 1.5 billion years after the Big Bang (Dec 2024), showing early galaxy structure formation


    Mapped Enceladus’ water plume structure, showing cascading cryovolcanic activity from Saturn’s moon


  • Significance:

    • Revolutionizing astrophysics and planetary science—from early-universe galaxies to exoplanet atmospheres and icy moon geophysics.
